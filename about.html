The Research Team

PhD Researcher

Clue Coman (they/them) is a PhD candidate and research assistant at the Centre for Social Research in Health, UNSW.


Their research explores the social imaginary of queer justice and examines the direct and social harms impacting LGBTQA+ people through the frameworks of critical human rights, queer theory, and zemiology. This thesis interrogates the justice gap between the legal and institutional protections for queer communities, and the reality of their lived experiences.


Research Areas: Social imaginaries of justice, Queer theory, Creative research methods.


Supervisory Team


Dr Anthony K J Smith (he/him) is the chief investigator and joint supervisor. Dr Smith has worked in LGBTQ+ communities for 10 years, and qualitative research for the last 7 years, and authored 40+ publications in LGBTQ+ health and social care.


Prof Christy Newman (they/them) is a joint supervisor. Prof Newman has worked in LGBTQ+ qualitative research for the last 20 years, and authored 100+ publications in LGBTQ+ health and social care.


Dr Andy Kaladelfos (they/them) is a co-supervisor on this project. Dr Kaladelfos has worked in LGBTQ+ communities for over 20 years, and specialises in LGBTQ+ criminology and qualitative research.
